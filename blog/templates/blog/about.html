Personal Introduction

I am hard working, eager to learn, communicatively strong, and always open for new opportunities. People I have worked with in the past have told me that they appreciate me because of my proactive and socially engaged attitude. During my physics education and industry career I developed my analytical thinking and approach to problem solving, as well as more practical skills such as programming and modelling. I'm flexible and motivated to apply my skills with a broad perspective.

Extracurricular Activies

IBM Q-challenge. (January 2019 – Present)

Together with a group of one professor and three students I participate in the IBM Q-challenge. This is a competitive challenge in which a lecture series in quantum computing is followed by a project in quantum computing. The team that engineers the best solution to a problem using quantum computing wins.

International Data Analysis Olympiad (IDAO). (January 2019 - February 2019)

Together with a professor and a student I participate in the IDAO. The goal of this olympiad is to create a Machine Learning (ML) algorithm that can handle track reconstruction in very high multiplicity environments. We are currently researching the applicability of novel machine learning models, as opposed to contemporary algorithms that are used in track reconstruction such as Kalman filters.

BrightHack Hackathon. (November 2018 - November 2018)

At the BrightHack hackathon I worked in a small team to develop soȅware that would streamline communications between police officers in the field and the criminal investigation department. This project included mainly natural language processing programming activities in python and MATLAB.

Simulating Heat Development in Microfluidic Chips Using Computer Aided Design (CAD). (June 2018 - July 2018)

Together with a group of four students I developed optimal microfluidic chip parameters in Fusion360, a CAD programme. This was done to aid a PhD student in his research.

Engineering a Genetic Algorithm Based on Fruit Fly Chemotaxis. (January 2018 - February 2018)

Together with a group of five students I developed a genetic algotithm based on the chemotactic behaviour of fruit fly larvae. I mainly worked in developing the genetic algorithm itself in MATLAB (and thus not so much in working out the biology).
